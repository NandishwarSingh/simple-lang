## Analysis (hand-written)

**This is the MIR optimizer release.** Per-pass attribution via benchmark
isolation (fib is pure inlining; collatz/spawnwork are pure strength
reduction; fold/DCE is their enabler):

| benchmark | v0.4 | v0.5 | pass responsible | prediction |
|-----------|-----:|-----:|------------------|-----------:|
| fib       | 0.63 (1.70x C) | **0.38 (1.06x)** | inlining (2-3 level recursion unroll) | 0.42 — beaten |
| collatz   | 1.65 (3.17x C) | **0.96 (1.88x)** | strength reduction | 0.70 — missed, see below |
| spawnwork | 0.33 (3.67x C) | **0.15 (1.67x)** | strength reduction (parallel) | 0.15 — exact |
| chanping  | 0.30 | **0.27 (1.00x C)** | inlining of hot helpers | — |
| primes / strbuild | — | unchanged (1.00x / 1.50x) | no regression | — |

- **fib now ties the C/Rust pack (1.06x) and beats Go by 1.5x.** The
  recursion-unrolling inliner cut ~8x of the call overhead, exactly the
  clang playbook.
- **collatz: every `div`/`rem` is gone** — `n % 2 == 0` compiles to one
  `and`, `n / 2` to the signed-shift fixup. The remaining 1.88x is loop
  scheduling/branch shape (clang emits branchless csel sequences), a
  machine-level matter that belongs to QBE, not MIR — consistent with our
  division-of-labor doctrine. Revisit only if a real workload cares.
- **A real bug caught by the harness:** first version of copy propagation
  assumed all temps single-assignment; inlined multi-return functions
  broke that (several `copy`s to one call-result temp). The differential
  test (`--no-opt` vs optimized must match on every example) caught it
  within minutes. That harness is now permanent.
- Zero-regression path held: `--no-opt` output is byte-identical to
  v0.4's emission on all 9 examples; ARC still leak-free optimized.
