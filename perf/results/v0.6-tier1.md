## Analysis (hand-written)

**Tier 1 of the optimization plan.** Geometric mean vs C: **1.86x → 1.45x**
(the plan predicted 1.48x).

| benchmark | before | after | change |
|-----------|-------:|------:|--------|
| bitops    | 13.00x | **1.00x** | 0.39 → 0.03 s — **13x faster, now level with C** |
| nbody     | 5.50x  | **4.40x** | bounds checks gone |
| fib       | 1.03x  | **1.00x** | |
| sortint   | 1.67x  | 1.67x | all bounds checks now gone (3 → 0) |
| others    | — | unchanged | |

### 1. Loop-idiom recognition — the headline

Two patterns, matched on the AST where the loop shape is still visible:

- **popcount**: `while (x != 0) { x = x & (x-1); n = n+1; }` becomes a
  branch-free 12-instruction SWAR sequence instead of one iteration per
  set bit. **bitops 0.39 → 0.03 s, exactly matching C.** This is what C
  was doing all along via clang's idiom recogniser — the 13x was never a
  codegen deficiency, just a missing pattern.
- **memset**: `for (i in lo..hi) { a[i] = c; }` becomes a `memset` call
  when the value is byte-uniform (any 1-byte element, or zero).

### 2. Bounds-check hoisting → post-loop range refinement

The general hoisting case turned out to be unnecessary once a sharper
fact was added: a loop guarded by `x >= k` that steps `x` down by at most
`c` must leave `x >= k - c` on exit — it could not have taken another
step without the guard passing first. That single rule proves
`a[j + 1]` safe after an insertion-sort inner loop. **sortint: 3 checks →
0.** Every benchmark now runs with zero bounds checks except where they
are genuinely needed.

### 3. Algebraic identities

`x+0`, `x*1`, `x*0`, `x&-1`, `x|0`, `x^x`, `x-x`, shifts by zero, and
friends. No measurable win alone, as expected — their job is to expose
constants for folding and DCE, and they cost nothing.

### 4. Loop unrolling — implemented, MEASURED, REMOVED

Full unrolling of short constant-trip-count loops looked free (the trip
count is in the syntax, the loop variable is immutable). It regressed
three benchmarks and improved none:

| benchmark | with unrolling | without |
|-----------|---------------:|--------:|
| nbody   | 0.36 | **0.22** |
| sieve   | 0.07 | **0.04** |
| sortint | 0.06 | **0.05** |

Cause: nested small loops compound. nbody's `for (i in 0..8)` containing
`for (j in 0..8)` unrolled to 64 copies of an inlined body and thrashed
the instruction cache. Out-of-order execution and modern branch
prediction have made small-loop unrolling far less valuable than the
textbooks suggest.

**Third pass deleted after measurement** (loop rotation, mimalloc, now
unrolling). The rule keeps paying for itself.
