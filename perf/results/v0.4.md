## Analysis (hand-written)

**This is the concurrency release.** Predictions from the design discussion
(recorded in internals ch7) vs reality:

- **chanping: Simple 0.30 s BEATS hand-written C/C++ pthreads (0.34 s) and
  ties Rust's mpsc (0.30 s).** Our channel runtime — emitted as QBE IR —
  outruns the same algorithm hand-written in C. Predicted "compete when
  buffered": exceeded.
- **Go's 0.02 s on chanping is the predicted loss**, and it's structural:
  goroutine parking (~100 ns) vs kernel thread wakeups (~µs). This is the
  price of OS threads, on the record; an M:N scheduler would erase it with
  zero syntax changes.
- **spawnwork: the parallelism works.** Simple 1.65 s → 0.33 s = 5.0x
  speedup on 8 workers (C scaled 5.8x). The 3.67x gap vs C is the *old*
  collatz scalar gap (no strength reduction — the MIR job), not a
  concurrency cost. Threading added essentially zero overhead.
- Memory: 1.4 MB with two live threads — Go carries 4.0 MB.
- All prior benchmarks unchanged. Both concurrency examples leak-free
  under `leaks --atExit`, including strings crossing threads and values
  left buffered in dropped channels (freed by the channel's element dtor).
